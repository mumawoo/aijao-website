Finance automation should start with roles, not one giant chatbot.

AIJAO is designed as a role-based finance intelligence system. Each assistant has a specific job, controlled permissions, accounting logic, and evidence requirements.

Phase 1

AP Clerk AI

Matches supplier invoices, CFDI tax XMLs, bank payments, and journal entries.

Phase 2

AR Clerk AI

Prioritizes collections, overdue invoices, and unmatched customer receipts.

Phase 3

Finance Manager AI

Aggregates AP, AR, cash, approvals, and exceptions into a daily control tower.

Phase 4

Controller AI

Supports close readiness, variance review, journal review, and audit evidence trails.

The moat is the accounting logic layer.

AIJAO is not just a chat interface. The system maps finance questions into controlled accounting definitions, matching rules, SQL queries, exception workflows, and evidence links.

1. Capture

Invoices, receipts, bank records, contracts, tax XMLs, and journal exports enter the system.

2. Understand

A financial semantic layer maps business language to accounts, vendors, rules, and metrics.

3. Match

Accounting logic connects invoices, payments, journal entries, approvals, and evidence.

4. Answer

Users receive a written answer, a source table, evidence references, and review actions.

Robotic document capture is the physical input layer.

AIJAO can integrate with a low-cost robotic invoice and document capture station that picks up, flattens, scans, classifies, and archives paper documents into the accounting workflow.

Pick up

Flexible fingers handle paper invoices, receipts, and supporting documents.

Scan and classify

OCR and finance rules identify vendor, amount, date, tax document, and document type.

Archive

Every document is linked to AP, AR, journal, contract, or audit workflows.

The founder's perspective combines accounting domain expertise with hands-on AI product building: turning messy finance work into auditable, role-based automation.
